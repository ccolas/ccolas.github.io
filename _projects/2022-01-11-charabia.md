What makes a word sound French? Not its meaning, just the way it rolls off the tongue. *Charabia* imagines French words that don't exist but feel like they should.

### Some favorites

The best invented words are the ones that seem to already mean something:

- *Climatum* — sounds like a contraction of climate and ultimatum
- *Fignotter* — a verb meaning "to meticulously refine," surely
- *Craquille* — something small and fragile that might crack; sounds like I could eat it too
- *Glandalourdi* — someone wonderfully sluggish
- *Couchottine* — a small, cozy sleeping place

### How it works

The idea is simple: using the 142,362 real French words from <a href="http://www.lexique.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Lexique.org</a>, learn which letters tend to follow with letter sequences, then generate new words by sampling new letters one by one following those patterns. This is called an <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-gram"  target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">N-gram model</a>. 

The key parameter is N, the number of preceding characters the model uses to make predictions about the following letter. Small N produces wilder, less recognizable words; large N stays closer to real French — so close that existing words start being generated.
